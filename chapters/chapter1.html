Introduction

Security system is a means or method by which something is served through a system of interworking components and devices. Security systems works on the same basic principle of securing entry points, like doors and windows, as well as interior space containing valuables (Retrieved from: www.safewise.com/home-security-faq/how-do-security-systems-work, Retrieved date: November 16, 2022). For instance, the home security system, its door locks. The door lock is the fastener fitted to a door or drawer to keep it firmly closed (Retrieved from: www.vocabulary.com/dictionary/doorlock, Retrieved date: November 16, 2022). Door lock has its two types: the Traditional lock & Smart lock. Traditional locks are what people refer to as typical locks; these locks need a physical key to open them. Smart locks are what people refer to as a lock that is controlled by a phone, or another device such as a fob. These locks use either Wi-Fi or Bluetooth and sometimes a physical key or ID card to unlock a door. (Retrieved from: www.keytek.co.uk/blog/smart-locks-vs-traditional-locks-2/, Retrieved date: October 5, 2021). Smart locks commonly use RFID technology and keypad door lock. RFID (radio frequency identification) is a form of wireless communication that incorporates the use of electromagnetic or electrostatic coupling in the radio frequency portion of the electromagnetic spectrum to uniquely identify an object, animal or person (Retrieved from: www.techtarget.com/iotagenda/definition/RFID-radio-frequency-identification Retrieved Date: November 21, 2022). Keypad door lock, this type of lock system requires a numerical code to grant entry to a facility or property. The code is punched in by users via a numerical pad, similar to those on a basic calculator. If the correct code is entered, the door lock or deadbolt should be released (Retrieved from: www.gvlock.com/blog/keypad-locks-101/, Retrieved date: November 16, 2022). In making the security tighter: researchers can combine those two, to Alarm system and SMS alert & authentication. Alarm systems can provide early warning of unauthorized access to your facilities by producing different kinds of sounds. (Retrieved from: www.protectivesecurity.govt.nz/physical-security /lifecycle/design/specific-security-measures/alarm-systems/, Retrieved date: November 22, 2022). SMS alert is a wireless communications solution to monitor alarms and control the remote equipment from anywhere, anytime (Retrieved from: www.allegro.com.sg/sms-alert-system/, Retrieve Date: November 14, 2022) while SMS authentication also known as SMS-based two-factor authentication (2FA) and SMS one-time password (OTP)—allows users to verify their identities with a code that is sent to them via text message (Retrieved from: www.okta.com/blog/2020/10/sms-authentication/, Retrieved date: October 26, 2020). These can only be possible by the use of Arduino, an open-source platform used for building electronics projects. Arduino consists of both a physical programmable circuit board (often referred to as a microcontroller) and a piece of software, or IDE (Integrated Development Environment) that runs on your computer, used to write and upload computer code to the physical board (Retrieved from: learn.sparkfun.com/tutorials/what-is-an-arduino/all Retrieved date: November 22, 2022).

Except for RFID technology, smart locks also commonly use keypad locks. According to Strauss, 2019 Keypad locks has the benefits of:

1. Convenience of a Keyless System. The biggest advantage is convenience. It eliminates the necessity of keys. There’s no need to carry them around, keep track of copies and spares, or change locks when a key is stolen or misplaced. Children, guests and service providers can be given their own code or a temporary code, rather than a key.

2. Higher Security. Keypad locks have a different hardware design than other types of locks. They don’t have cylinder mechanisms, so they essentially cannot be picked or bumped like conventional locks. This keeps the building more secure. Instead of risking losing keys and having to rekey or change out the lock. With a keypad system, it’s easy to change the security pin code whenever necessary.

3. Enhanced Durability. Traditional keys and locks wear down over time; it becomes more difficult to get it to turn and function properly. Keypads are much more durable than conventional locks. A high-quality lock and keypad device should last longer with standard hardware.

4. Easy Installation. Many property owners think that if they want to install a keypad lock, they’ll have to make major adjustments to the door or frames. That’s not true. In most cases, keypad locks can be easily installed on standard doors, just like any standard lock. Mechanical and battery-powered keypad locks are available which don’t require connection to a power source.

5. Integration with Access Control Systems. Keypad systems can be used as part of an access control system. This allows companies to restrict entry permission, even when using the correct code, during certain hours of the day or night. Businesses can also place a keypad entry system within the interior of a building. This restricts access to certain parts of the facility. It’s handy for business owners who want to limit employee access to certain parts of an office complex during certain times of the day.

As for SMS authentication it has its pros listed below according to Shyamsundar (2020):

1.More secure than passwords alone. Passwords are inherently weak because users tend to forget them, recycle them across various accounts, or have them stolen due to poor storage practices (e.g., writing them on a sticky note). SMS authentication helps to minimize our reliance on passwords, and makes it more difficult for bad actors to steal logins and hack accounts.

2.Convenience. One of the reasons users recycle passwords is because of the sheer volume of online accounts they create and manage: our research shows that people have to remember 10 passwords every day. SMS authentication removes this hassle as it sends unique codes directly to the user, which they can then easily input on a website or app to verify their identities.

3.Better than no 2FA. Proving an identity with more than one piece of information is always going to be more secure than proving it with a single factor. SMS authentication, therefore, is a safer alternative.

Research Project

General Objectives

To design and implement the RFID, Keypad lock, and SMS Security: an Arduino-Based with Alert System.

The Researchers main objective is to develop a security system with the design and implementation of RFID, Keypad lock, and SMS security: an Arduin-based with Alarm system that has the functionality of notifying the user via text message whenever there is someone tried to force their way in on the user’s property. The proposed study can automatically notify the owners when some certain circumstances have met, generates OTP, produces an alarming sound, and automatically closes it after 10 seconds.

2.To make a module that will automatically send a SMS to the user whenever some circumstance has happened on the system. The system will automatically send a SMS to the user whenever there are some circumstances happening on their properties.

Scopes and Limitations

Scopes

1.The system will display on the LCD if the RFID card and OTP is valid or invalid.
2.The system will need two (2) keys, RFID card and OTP. The absence of one, denied the access.
3.The system will generate a unique code every time it detects the registered RFID cards.
4.The system will deliver a message via SMS to the registered RFID card’s mobile number/s, to notify the user/s whenever it opens and gets subjected to burglary.
5.There are designated RFID cards in every registered phone number.
6.The system has a feature that can open and close the lock of the door manually inside the house using button.
7.The system can be open from the inside of the room using switch button.

Limitations

1.The system’s RFID reader will only accept registered tags.
2.The system’s log is the text messages the user received from the system itself.
3.The system is also limited in its range; notifications can no longer be delivered in places that have no signal.
4.The text messages are limited, depends on how much prepaid load the user puts on it.
5.The users cannot register RFID cards and phone numbers by themselves.
6.The users do not have the visibility of the list of the registered RFID tags and phone numbers.
7.The system can't be used if the users don’t have electricity.
8.The users can’t backtrack the unique identifier of the RFID and the wrong combination of pin code.
9.The user will not be notified if the system has no prepaid load balance.
